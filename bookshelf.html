Book Review: Psychology of Money.

Author: Morgan Housel, Pradnes Gujrathi.
Genre: Personal Finance / Psychology.
Published: 2020.
Pages: 252
⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)


The Psychology of Money
explores how emotions, biases, and behavior shape our financial decisions—often more than pure logic and technical knowledge. Morgan Housel shares timeless lessons through compelling storytelling, drawing on history, psychology, and his own experiences.

One of its key insights is that financial success doesn't depend on knowing complex formulas, but on how you behave—how patient, frugal, and consistent you are. The book emphasizes the power of long-term thinking and the unpredictability of markets, making it a must-read not just for investors but anyone who interacts with money.

This book is a refreshing take on wealth-building that celebrates humility, self-awareness, and the emotional intelligence behind financial decisions. Highly recommended for beginners and experts alike. Read It If You Are A Barca Fan.

Book Review: Naruto Manga.

Author: Masashi Kishimoto., Pradnesh Gujrathi.
Genre: Entertainment. / Funn.
Published: 1999.
Pages: Series.
⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)


Naruto is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Masashi Kishimoto. It tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja who seeks recognition from his peers and dreams of becoming the Hokage, the leader of his village. The story is told in two parts: the first is set in Naruto's pre-teen years (volumes 1–27), and the second in his teens (volumes 28–72). The series is based on two one-shot manga by Kishimoto: Karakuri (1995), which earned Kishimoto an honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly Hop Step Award the following year, and Naruto (1997).

Book Review: One Piece.

Author: Eiichiro Oda., Pradnesh Gujrathi.
Genre: Entertainment. / Funn.
Published: July 1997.
Pages: Series.
⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)


One Piece (stylized in all caps) is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Eiichiro Oda. It follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy and his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, as he explores the Grand Line in search of the mythical treasure known as the "One Piece" to become the next King of the Pirates. The manga has been serialized in Shueisha's shōnen manga magazine Weekly Shōnen Jump since July 1997, with its chapters compiled in 111 tankōbon volumes as of March 2025. It was licensed for an English language release in North America and the United Kingdom by Viz Media and in Australia by Madman Entertainment. Becoming a media franchise, it has been adapted into a festival film by Production I.G, and an anime series by Toei Animation, which began broadcasting in 1999. Additionally, Toei has developed 14 animated feature films and one original video animation. Several companies have developed various types of merchandising and media, such as a trading card game and video games. Netflix released a live action TV series adaptation in 2023.
